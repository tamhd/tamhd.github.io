EXPERIENCE

What I do. What I did.

My daily job involves big data processing (clean, display, analysis, etc), Machine Learning and occasionally production. I have experience in Fraud Detection, text mining and especially Natural Language Processing.

MT

Machine Translation
The task when non-MT people constantly tell me: "Why bother doing it, useless, you would never beat Google Translate". Hmm, I disagree.

I was heavily involved in Statistical MT for around 2 years, primarily phrase-based approaches. Now I am excited again because of Neural MT.

GEC

Grammatical Error Correction

Research into the possibility of automatically correct written English. Mainly, the task was formulated as 'translating erroneous input into proper English' (thanks to MT).

Competing with state-of-the-art solution and tier-1 NLP groups worldwide was a thrilling experience.

Fraud Detection

Fall in the same category as outlier detection, anomaly detection, etc. It is a pain to every internet company, and each company has its own definition. I participated in not only machine learning modeling, system production but also operational activities.

Fun fact: unlike other projects, this is not related to NLP.


Sentiment Analysis


Sentiment Analysis, or Opinion mining, the thing most people care about what other are saying. My project was to predict a movie review as 'positive' or 'negative' using Machine Learning. It was a document-level sentiment analysis with just two classes.

It is in fact a very important tasks nowadays regarding news, feedback, reviews, etc.

IR

Information Retrieval


It is about giving users the most relevant documents. Not only is IR an important field on its own, but it also helps other task.

I come across IR not one, but three times. Each time I needed to use a different tactics: string edit distance, vector space model and Lucene/Solr library.

QA

Question Answering


While IR tries to return relevant documents, QA being more advanced tries to answer the question in customer's mind. It is indeed a difficult topic of Natural Language Generation.

From 2010 to 2012, my methods were rule-based (hand-crafted macro based on GATE regular expression) and knowledge-based (Ontology). However, the field has changed dramatically since.
